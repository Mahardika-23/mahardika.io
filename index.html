About

McDonald's Corporation is an American fast food company, founded in 1940 as a restaurant operated by Richard and Maurice McDonald, in San Bernardino, California, USA. They reorganized their business as a hamburger stand and later turned the company into a franchise, with the Golden Arches logo introduced in 1953 at a location in Phoenix, Arizona. In 1955, Ray Kroc, a businessman, joined the company as a franchise agent and started buying the chain from the McDonald brothers. McDonald's originally had its headquarters in Oak Brook, Illinois, but moved its global headquarters to Chicago in June 2018.
